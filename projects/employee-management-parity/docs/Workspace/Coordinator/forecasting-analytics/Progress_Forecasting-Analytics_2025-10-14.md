# Forecasting & Analytics Parity – Naumen vs Demo

This report examines the **Forecasting & Analytics** slice of the Naumen WFM system and compares it against the new forecasting demo hosted at [https://forecasting-analytics-4pfj6jygr-granins-projects.vercel.app](https://forecasting-analytics-4pfj6jygr-granins-projects.vercel.app). All observations were made on 14 October 2025 using the OIDC login provided by the customer (`user20082025`) and the official manuals (CH2–CH4). Behavioural parity is the focus; visual details such as colours, line styles or fonts are out of scope.

## Conclusions & parity plan

The current forecasting & analytics demo covers only a subset of the real system’s forecasting functionality. It offers **model analytics**, **trend analysis** with a confidence band and **manual adjustments**, but it omits critical workflows such as building forecasts, setting untypical days, absenteeism calculation, and data exports. Conversely, the real Naumen interface exposes build/exceptions/trends/absenteeism tabs but lacks aggregated KPI dashboards or confidence bands. Manual editing of forecasts is described in the documentation but not yet visible in the real build; the demo includes a prototype of this feature.

To reach 100 % behavioural parity:

1. **Integrate full forecasting workflows into the demo** – add pages for building forecasts (queue selection, horizon input, FTE chart), setting exceptions, and calculating absenteeism. Ensure that all fields and buttons match RU wording from CH4 (e.g., “Период построения”, “Исторический горизонт”, “Загрузить прогноз/факт/абсентеизм”, “Построить”).
    
2. **Include real data filters** – implement queue selectors and historical/forecast period pickers to match the production interface. Currently, demo charts use mocked data and do not allow selecting organisational points.
    
3. **Export support** – provide export icons or buttons to download forecast tables as Excel files, aligning with CH4 and CH6 instructions.
    
4. **Align accuracy metrics** – if KPI cards are retained, define how MAPE/MAE/RMSE are calculated and wire them to real forecast/actual data. Otherwise, reflect the real system’s deviation tables instead of introducing unsupported metrics.
    
5. **Enhance trend analysis** – ensure that seasonality and anomaly blocks are editable, and add optional smoothing tools and historical exclusions as described in the manual.
    
6. **Absenteeism** – implement absenteeism profile creation and import functions (absenteeism templates) so analysts can replicate the workflows in CH4 §4.3.
    

By following these steps and cross‑referencing with Chapter 4 of the manual, the forecasting demo can evolve from a proof‑of‑concept into a true mirror of the Naumen forecasting module.
