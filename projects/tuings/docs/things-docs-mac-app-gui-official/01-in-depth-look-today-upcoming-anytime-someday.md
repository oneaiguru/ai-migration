# An In-Depth Look at Today, Upcoming, Anytime, and Someday

The default, date-based lists in Things allow you to control when tasks come to your attention.



In **Things**, you can look at your to-dos from two different perspectives: The first is *context*, provided by areas and projects that you create yourself. The other way to view to-dos is by looking at *when* you are going to do them.

Things provides several default lists that help you plan the current day, and what you want to achieve in the future. This page explains the purpose of each of these default lists.

## Today & This Evening

**Today** is the list for to-dos that you want to start before the day ends. They‚Äôre your priorities.

![The Today list in Things, including the optional This Evening section and calendar events at the top.](https://culturedcode.com/frozen/2025/10/dates-today.jpg)

Ideally, start the day by quickly reviewing the list ‚Äì **Today** works best if it contains only those to-dos you‚Äòll work on today. Then, drag and drop to-dos into the order you‚Äôll most likely tackle them. Pick whatever is at the top when you come back throughout the day.

If something needs to be handled later in the day, assign it to **This Evening**. These to-dos will move into their own section at the bottom ‚Äì still present, so you know there‚Äôs more to do, but unobtrusive enough to not bother you until you have time.

## Upcoming

The **Upcoming** list is your future agenda: a place for tasks that you can't tackle right now but want to conquer on a specific day down the road.

At the top of **Upcoming**, you‚Äôll see the next seven days listed separately, starting with tomorrow. ## Anytime

As the name implies, the content in **Anytime** can be tackled ‚Äúat any time‚Äù ‚Äì nothing‚Äôs blocking you from working on any task in this list right now. Because the list contains only *active* to-dos, its contents and what you see in **Upcoming** and **Someday** are mutually exclusive.

![The Anytime list in Things shows all active to-dos which can be tackled at any time.](https://culturedcode.com/frozen/2025/10/dates-anytime.jpg)

The order of to-dos in **Anytime** reflects the sidebar/main lists view: loose to-dos without a parent (project or area ) float at the top, and then to-dos are grouped under their direct parent list. ## Someday

All the other date-based lists contain to-dos that you have a very clear vision for: they are ready to be tackled at any time, or when their start date arrives.

Why, then, would you keep something so vague around? Well, let‚Äôs be honest ‚Äì we don‚Äôt always have all the answers right away, or the time to look for them.
